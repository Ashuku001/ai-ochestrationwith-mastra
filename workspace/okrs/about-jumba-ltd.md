## 2. Mission & Vision

**Mission:**
> Jumba is the trusted digital partner for hardware stores. Through tech, we support hardware stores to grow.

**Vision:**
> To make Jumba the source of all construction materials in Kenya — and eventually beyond its borders — while contributing to solving Africa's housing deficit through technology.

**North Star Problem:**
Africa's construction industry is a $10 trillion market that is largely undigitised, fragmented, and riddled with supply chain inefficiencies. Kenya alone faces an 80% housing deficit. Jumba exists to fix the infrastructure that feeds construction — starting with hardware stores.

---

## 3. What Jumba Does

Jumba is a **B2B construction technology platform** that digitises and simplifies the construction materials supply chain in Kenya. It sits between **manufacturers** and **hardware stores / construction sites**, removing friction at every layer.

### Core Products

#### 📦 Jumba Marketplace (App)
- Hardware stores order cement and construction materials directly via the Jumba app
- Standardised, negotiated pricing across top cement brands (Simba, Mombasa, Rhino, Blue Triangle, Bamburi, etc.)
- Reliable last-mile delivery across Kenya
- 1,350+ hardware stores onboarded; 240,000+ orders processed; 12.6M+ cement bags sold
- 135+ hardware stores use the app daily

#### 💳 Jengwa (Embedded Credit / BNPL)
- Buy Now Pay Later product for hardware stores to access inventory on credit
- Addresses cash flow constraints that have historically blocked small hardware stores from stocking up
- Integrated with bank partners: M-Pesa, Equity, DTB, NCBA

#### 🖥️ Jumba OS
- Business management software for hardware stores
- Covers: sales tracking, stock management, creditor management
- Positions Jumba not just as a supplier but as an operating system for the hardware store business

---

## 4. Business Model

- **Revenue:** Margin on construction materials sold through the platform (negotiates discounted prices with manufacturers, adds markup)
- **Financial Services:** Credit product (Jengwa) — embedded BNPL layered on top of the marketplace
- **Enterprise Play (emerging):** Long-term credit for real estate developers to finance project materials

## 7. Competitive Positioning

- **Against traditional distributors:** Jumba offers standardised pricing, digital ordering, and reliable delivery — removing the need to negotiate with multiple suppliers
- **Against informal restocking:** Hardware stores previously relied on fragmented, offline sourcing from multiple manufacturers with no price transparency
- **Moat:** Distribution network, manufacturer relationships, embedded credit (Jengwa), and now Jumba OS which creates operational lock-in

## 10. Strategic Priorities (Inferred)

Based on public statements and product evolution, Jumba's current strategic bets are:

1. **Deepen Kenya penetration** — Reach remaining 40% of counties before expanding regionally
2. **Grow Jengwa (credit)** — Embedded finance is the highest-margin layer; scaling BNPL to more hardware stores and introducing developer credit
3. **Jumba OS adoption** — Converting transactional customers into fully managed OS users creates retention and data moat
4. **Expand beyond cement** — Cement is the entry product; full construction materials catalogue is the vision
5. **Regional expansion** — East Africa is the next frontier once Kenya is consolidated

## 11. Context for OKR Strategy

When crafting OKRs for Jumba, agents should:

- **Always anchor to the mission:** Jumba's OKRs must trace back to hardware store growth and construction supply chain efficiency
- **Distinguish between business lines:** Marketplace OKRs, Jengwa (credit) OKRs, and Jumba OS OKRs may have different success metrics
- **Consider the B2B2C nature:** Jumba's growth is measured both by hardware store acquisition/retention AND the downstream volume those stores generate
- **Watch the credit risk angle:** Jengwa introduces financial risk — governance OKRs must account for credit quality, not just disbursement volume
- **Kenya first, region second:** Any OKR referencing geographic expansion should treat Kenya consolidation as a prerequisite
